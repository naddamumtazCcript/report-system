# **BE BALANCED – LAB LIBRARY FOUNDATIONS**

*(AI Reference & Mapping Rules)*

## **PURPOSE OF THIS LIBRARY**

This library exists to guide AI in consistently translating:

* client intake data  
* lab results  
* symptom patterns

into a **clear, educational, client-facing protocol**, without diagnosing or inventing information.

The AI must **map patterns**, not make medical claims.

---

## **CORE RULES FOR AI (NON-NEGOTIABLE)**

1. **Never diagnose**  
   * Use pattern-based language (e.g., “consistent with,” “may contribute to”).  
2. **Use intake language verbatim**  
   * Match client-stated symptoms word-for-word where possible.  
3. **Do not invent labs or symptoms**  
   * If not present, mark as *“Not reported.”*  
4. **Labs inform priorities, not overwhelm**  
   * Highlight the *fewest* markers needed to explain the strategy.  
5. **Every lab finding must map to action**  
   * Nutrition, lifestyle, supplements, or testing.  
6. **Always explain “why this matters”**  
   * In 1–3 sentences, client-friendly, non-clinical.  
7. **Protocols are phase-based**  
   * Focus on *what matters now*, not everything at once.

---

## **STANDARD PROTOCOL FLOW (ALWAYS FOLLOW)**

1. Education Resource (video)  
2. Focus Items (high-level actions)  
3. Top Concerns (symptoms \+ likely drivers)

## **LAB → PATTERN → ACTION MAPPING RULES**

### **ESTROGEN — LOW**

**Interpretation Pattern**

* Low estrogen may be influenced by lifestyle factors, supplements (e.g., DIM), stress, or lack of ovulation.

**Why This Matters (Standard Language)**

* Estrogen supports mood, libido, sleep quality, and healthy menstrual flow. Low levels can affect how other hormones function.

**Common Symptoms to Reference**

* Low libido  
* Poor sleep  
* Mood changes  
* Cycle changes

**Protocol Mapping**

* Avoid overly aggressive estrogen-lowering strategies  
* Support ovulation and progesterone first  
* Emphasize balanced nutrition and stress reduction

---

### **PROGESTERONE — LOW / NOT OVULATING**

**Interpretation Pattern**

* Low progesterone is often consistent with anovulatory cycles or luteal phase disruption.

**Why This Matters**

* Progesterone helps calm the nervous system, support sleep, reduce cramping, and balance estrogen.

**Symptoms to Map**

* Sleep disruption  
* Increased stress sensitivity  
* Cramping  
* Heavy bleeding

**Protocol Mapping**

* Prioritize ovulation support  
* Avoid driving estrogen too low  
* Support stress resilience and blood sugar balance

---

### **ANDROGEN DOMINANCE (PCOS PATTERN)**

**Interpretation Pattern**

* Elevated androgens relative to estrogen/progesterone may be consistent with PCOS physiology.

**Why This Matters**

* High androgens can suppress ovulation, lower progesterone production, and contribute to hormonal imbalance.

**Symptoms to Map**

* Acne (jawline, chin, cheeks)  
* Hair shedding  
* Cycle irregularity  
* Low libido

**Protocol Mapping**

* Blood sugar regulation is a top priority  
* Consider inositol-based support  
* Include androgen-modulating strategies (nutrition \+ supplements)

---

### **METHYLATION — LOW**

**Interpretation Pattern**

* Reduced methylation capacity may affect hormone clearance and neurotransmitter balance.

**Why This Matters**

* Methylation helps clear hormones, regulate mood, and support detoxification.

**Symptoms to Map**

* Mood changes  
* Hormone recirculation  
* Poor stress tolerance

**Protocol Mapping**

* Support detox pathways gently  
* Avoid overload  
* Reinforce nutrient sufficiency (B vitamins)

---

### **PHASE 2 ESTROGEN METABOLISM — SLOW**

**Interpretation Pattern**

* Sluggish estrogen packaging for elimination, even when total estrogen is low.

**Why This Matters**

* Poor Phase 2 detox can lead to estrogen recirculation and hormonal discomfort.

**Symptoms to Map**

* Bloating  
* Cramping  
* Heavy periods

**Protocol Mapping**

* Emphasize fiber intake  
* Support liver detox pathways  
* Avoid aggressive estrogen suppression

---

### **CORTISOL METABOLISM — LOW CLEARANCE**

**Interpretation Pattern**

* Cortisol production may be present, but breakdown and clearance are impaired.

**Why This Matters**

* Poor cortisol metabolism disrupts sleep-wake rhythms, stress tolerance, and metabolic health.

**Symptoms to Map**

* Poor stress tolerance  
* Sleep disruption  
* Difficulty losing weight

**Protocol Mapping**

* Prioritize nervous system regulation  
* Avoid overtraining  
* Reinforce circadian rhythm habits

---

### **GLUTATHIONE — LOW END OF RANGE**

**Interpretation Pattern**

* Reduced antioxidant capacity and detox support.

**Why This Matters**

* Glutathione is the body’s primary antioxidant and supports hormone clearance.

**Symptoms to Map**

* Increased inflammation  
* Slower estrogen detox  
* Oxidative stress

**Protocol Mapping**

* Gentle detox support  
* Emphasize antioxidant-rich foods  
* Consider NAC / glycine when appropriate

---

### **VITAMIN B6 — FUNCTIONAL DEFICIENCY**

**Interpretation Pattern**

* Functional B6 deficiency may impair progesterone production and neurotransmitter balance.

**Why This Matters**

* B6 is required for hormone metabolism, mood regulation, and sleep quality.

**Symptoms to Map**

* Mood changes  
* Sleep disturbances

**Protocol Mapping**

* Nutrient repletion  
* Support progesterone pathways  
* Reinforce food-first strategies

---

## **NUTRITION RULES (GLOBAL)**

* Blood sugar balance is **foundational**, especially in PCOS patterns  
* Always recommend:  
  * Protein first  
  * No naked carbs  
  * Fiber-forward meals  
* Macro adjustments should be:  
  * Framed as *supportive*, not restrictive  
  * Time-bound (e.g., 12-week phase)

---

## **SUPPLEMENT RULES (GLOBAL)**

* Supplements are **supportive, not primary**  
* Pause anything actively worsening labs (e.g., DIM lowering estrogen too far)  
* Always include:  
  * Purpose  
  * Duration  
  * Re-evaluation point  
* Never imply supplements replace medical care

---

## **TIMELINE & EXPECTATION RULES**

* Improvement is gradual  
* Early changes: weeks 3–4  
* Meaningful shifts: 8–12+ weeks  
* Normalize non-linear healing
