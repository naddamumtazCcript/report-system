# **BE BALANCED – FITNESS & LIFESTYLE LIBRARY FOUNDATIONS**

*(AI Reference & Mapping Rules)*

## **PURPOSE OF THIS LIBRARY**

This library guides AI in translating:

* client intake data  
* lab patterns  
* symptom priorities

into a **supportive, sustainable fitness and lifestyle strategy** that enhances hormone health, metabolic function, stress resilience, and long-term adherence.

Movement and lifestyle are framed as **regulators of physiology**, not calorie-burning or productivity tools.

---

## **CORE FITNESS & LIFESTYLE RULES FOR AI (NON-NEGOTIABLE)**

1. **Recovery \> intensity**  
   * Especially when hormones, digestion, or stress are compromised.  
2. **Consistency beats optimization**  
   * Simple, repeatable routines outperform complex plans.  
3. **Strength training is foundational**  
   * Especially for PCOS, insulin resistance, and metabolic health.  
4. **Walking is non-negotiable**  
   * Daily low-intensity movement supports glucose control and stress regulation.  
5. **Nervous system regulation is required**  
   * Not optional when cortisol, sleep, or mood are affected.  
6. **Avoid extremes**  
   * No overtraining, excessive HIIT, or rigid schedules.  
7. **Lifestyle supports fitness, not the other way around**  
   * Sleep, stress, and circadian rhythm come first.  
8. **Everything is phase-dependent**  
   * Fitness and lifestyle intensity must match the client’s current capacity.

## **FITNESS → PATTERN → ACTION MAPPING RULES**

### **1️⃣ PCOS / BLOOD SUGAR / METABOLIC SUPPORT**

**Trigger Signals**

* PCOS pattern  
* Insulin resistance  
* Energy crashes  
* Weight loss resistance

**Movement Strategy**

* Strength training \+ walking  
* Minimal high-intensity cardio

**Standard Mapping Language**

* “Supports insulin sensitivity and metabolic health.”  
* “Builds lean muscle to improve glucose uptake.”

**Rules**

* 3–4 strength sessions per week max  
* Emphasize compound lifts  
* Avoid fasted workouts

---

### **2️⃣ HIGH STRESS / CORTISOL DYSREGULATION**

**Trigger Signals**

* Poor stress tolerance  
* Sleep disruption  
* Cortisol metabolism issues

**Movement Strategy**

* Low to moderate intensity  
* Shorter sessions  
* Recovery-focused

**Standard Mapping Language**

* “Supports nervous system regulation.”  
* “Avoids additional stress load.”

**Rules**

* Limit HIIT or intense conditioning  
* Prioritize walking, mobility, and breathwork  
* No “push through fatigue” messaging

---

### **3️⃣ LOW ENERGY / FATIGUE / BURNOUT**

**Trigger Signals**

* Chronic fatigue  
* Low motivation  
* Poor recovery

**Movement Strategy**

* Maintain movement without depletion

**Rules**

* Reduce volume before intensity  
* Encourage shorter, more frequent sessions  
* Normalize rest days

---

### **4️⃣ GUT HEALING / DIGESTIVE PHASE**

**Trigger Signals**

* Bloating  
* GI discomfort  
* Elimination diet phase

**Movement Strategy**

* Gentle, non-jarring movement

**Rules**

* Walking, light strength, yoga  
* Avoid excessive core bracing or high-impact work  
* Emphasize parasympathetic activation

---

## **DAILY MOVEMENT RULES (GLOBAL)**

* **Walking target:** 8,000–10,000 steps/day (as tolerated)  
* Walking after meals is encouraged for blood sugar support  
* Outdoor walking preferred when possible

---

## **STRENGTH TRAINING RULES (GLOBAL)**

* Frequency: 2–4× per week depending on phase  
* Splits:  
  * 2 upper / 2 lower  
  * OR 2 total body  
* Rest days are mandatory  
* Progressive overload only when recovery is adequate

---

## **LIFESTYLE & NERVOUS SYSTEM SUPPORT RULES**

### **Stress Regulation (Required When Indicated)**

AI must include at least **one daily nervous system practice** when stress markers are present:

* Breathwork  
* Meditation  
* Stretching  
* Gentle yoga  
* Time outdoors  
* Digital boundaries

Language must be:

* supportive  
* optional-feeling, not prescriptive

---

## **SLEEP & CIRCADIAN RHYTHM RULES**

**When Sleep Is Compromised**  
AI must prioritize:

* Consistent sleep/wake times  
* Morning light exposure (within 30 minutes of waking)  
* Reduced blue light exposure at night  
* Calm evening routines

**Standard Sleep Language**

* “Sleep is foundational for hormone and metabolic health.”  
* “Small improvements compound over time.”

---

## **BEHAVIORAL GUARDRAILS (IMPORTANT)**

AI must explicitly flag when appropriate:

* Avoid excessive HIIT  
* Avoid fasted workouts  
* Avoid late-night intense training  
* Avoid screen use before bed  
* Avoid over-scheduling workouts

Guardrails should be framed as **protective**, not restrictive.

---

## **PHASE-BASED INTENSITY RULES**

| Phase | Fitness Focus | Lifestyle Focus |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Gut / Reset | Gentle strength \+ walking | Stress reduction, sleep |
| Hormone | Strength-focused, moderate | Circadian rhythm |
| Metabolic | Progressive strength | Consistency, steps |
| Maintenance | Enjoyment-based | Sustainability |

---

## **CLIENT LANGUAGE RULES**

AI must:

* Avoid “burn calories” framing  
* Avoid body-shaming or urgency  
* Emphasize how movement supports hormones, mood, and energy  
* Normalize fluctuating capacity

Movement and lifestyle recommendations are educational and should be adapted based on individual capacity, injury history, and medical guidance. Clients should consult their healthcare provider before starting new exercise routines.
