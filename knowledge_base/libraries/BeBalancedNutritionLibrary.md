# **BE BALANCED – NUTRITION LIBRARY FOUNDATIONS**

*(AI Reference & Mapping Rules)*

## **PURPOSE OF THIS LIBRARY**

This library guides AI in translating:

* client intake data  
* lab patterns  
* symptom priorities

into a **clear, supportive, and actionable nutrition strategy**, without prescribing, diagnosing, or over-restricting.

Nutrition is framed as a **tool to support physiology**, not as a diet.

---

## **CORE NUTRITION RULES FOR AI (NON-NEGOTIABLE)**

1. **Food is supportive, not punitive**  
   * Never frame nutrition as restriction or “cutting out forever.”  
2. **Blood sugar balance is foundational**  
   * Especially when PCOS, androgen dominance, fatigue, or cortisol issues are present.  
3. **Protein anchors every meal**  
   * Always prioritize protein first.  
4. **No naked carbohydrates**  
   * Carbohydrates must be paired with protein and/or fat.  
5. **Fiber is introduced progressively**  
   * Increase gradually to avoid bloating.  
6. **Simplicity over complexity**  
   * Fewer ingredients, repeatable meals.  
7. **Nutrition strategy must match the phase**  
   * Elimination → support → reintroduction.  
8. **Macros are optional, not mandatory**  
   * Always provide an intuitive fallback (Plate Method).

---

## **STANDARD NUTRITION FLOW (ALWAYS FOLLOW)**

1. Primary Nutrition Goal  
2. Diet Framework  
3. Why This Helps (brief)  
4. Core Habits  
5. Macro Guidance (if applicable)  
6. Plate Method (if not tracking)  
7. Recommended Foods  
8. Foods to Limit (phase-specific)

## **NUTRITION → PATTERN → ACTION MAPPING RULES**

### **1️⃣ BLOOD SUGAR DYSREGULATION / PCOS PATTERN**

**Trigger Signals**

* PCOS diagnosis or pattern  
* Elevated androgens  
* Energy crashes  
* Cravings  
* Difficulty losing weight

**Nutrition Strategy**

* Slightly lower-carbohydrate, protein-forward  
* Even carb distribution across meals  
* Emphasis on fiber and fat pairing

**Core Habits**

* Protein first  
* No naked carbs  
* 10-minute walk after meals  
* Optional CGM for feedback

**Macro Guidance**

* Moderate carbs (not keto)  
* Adequate protein (≥25–40g per meal)

---

### **2️⃣ BLOATING / DIGESTIVE SYMPTOMS**

**Trigger Signals**

* Daily bloating  
* Gas  
* Morning abdominal discomfort  
* Irregular digestion

**Nutrition Strategy**

* Elimination-style, low-reactivity foods  
* Gradual fiber introduction  
* Simple meals

**Core Habits**

* Fewer ingredients per meal  
* Thorough chewing  
* Avoid large raw salads initially

**Food Focus**

* Gluten-free  
* Dairy-free  
* Low-FODMAP leaning (initially)

---

### **3️⃣ HORMONE CLEARANCE / ESTROGEN SUPPORT**

**Trigger Signals**

* Heavy periods  
* Cramping  
* Estrogen metabolism markers slow  
* Bloating around cycle

**Nutrition Strategy**

* Fiber-forward  
* Cruciferous vegetables as tolerated  
* Support liver detox pathways

**Food Focus**

* Broccoli sprouts  
* Leafy greens  
* Berries  
* Ground flax (if tolerated)

---

### **4️⃣ LOW ENERGY / FATIGUE / STRESS LOAD**

**Trigger Signals**

* Poor stress tolerance  
* Sleep disruption  
* Cortisol metabolism issues

**Nutrition Strategy**

* Consistent meal timing  
* Adequate carbohydrates  
* Avoid aggressive calorie restriction

**Core Habits**

* Regular meals  
* Evening carbohydrate inclusion if sleep is disrupted  
* Adequate sodium and hydration

---

## **MACRO RULES (GLOBAL)**

* Macros are **tools**, not goals  
* Used when:  
  * weight loss is a stated goal  
  * structure improves compliance  
* Avoid macro tracking when:  
  * stress is high  
  * history of disordered eating exists

**Standard Macro Language**

* “These targets provide structure while remaining flexible.”  
* “Consistency matters more than perfection.”

---

## **PLATE METHOD RULES (INTUITIVE OPTION)**

When macros are not used, AI must default to:

* ½ plate non-starchy vegetables  
* ¼ plate protein  
* ¼ plate carbohydrates  
* Add fats intentionally

This method should always be offered as an alternative.

---

## **ELIMINATION PHASE NUTRITION RULES**

**Purpose**

* Reduce inflammation  
* Calm digestion  
* Identify triggers

**Duration**

* Typically 3–4 weeks unless otherwise specified

**Allowed Framework**

* Gluten-free  
* Dairy-free  
* Whole foods  
* Low-reactivity proteins

**Reintroduction Rule**

* Reintroduce **one food category at a time**  
* Monitor symptoms before progressing

---

## **FOOD LIST LOGIC (FOR AI)**

When generating food lists:

* Group by **protein / carbs / fats / fiber**  
* Limit to **10–20 foods per category**  
* Prefer foods already referenced in Be Balanced protocols  
* Flag optional foods as “if tolerated”

---

## **CLIENT LANGUAGE RULES**

AI must:

* Use encouraging, neutral tone  
* Avoid fear-based food language  
* Avoid absolutes (“never,” “always”)  
* Normalize flexibility and progress over perfection

---

## **NUTRITION DISCLAIMERS (STANDARD)**

Nutrition guidance must always be framed as:

* Educational  
* Non-medical  
* Adjustable based on tolerance
