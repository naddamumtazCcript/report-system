# **BE BALANCED – SUPPLEMENT LIBRARY FOUNDATIONS**

*(AI Reference & Mapping Rules)*

## **PURPOSE OF THIS LIBRARY**

This library guides AI in translating:

* client intake data  
* lab patterns  
* symptom priorities

into a **clear, educational, and supportive supplement protocol**, without diagnosing, prescribing, or implying supplements replace medical care.

Supplements are framed as **adjuncts**, not solutions.

---

## **CORE SUPPLEMENT RULES FOR AI (NON-NEGOTIABLE)**

1. **Food and lifestyle come first**  
   * Supplements support physiology; they do not replace habits.  
2. **Fewer supplements \> more supplements**  
   * Prioritize clarity, compliance, and tolerance.  
3. **Every supplement must have a purpose**  
   * No “just in case” additions.  
4. **Duration must be stated**  
   * Ongoing vs time-bound vs re-evaluate.  
5. **Pause before adding**  
   * If a supplement may be contributing to imbalance, flag it first.  
6. **One new supplement at a time**  
   * Especially in sensitive or GI-focused clients.  
7. **Never imply treatment or cure**  
   * Use educational, supportive language only.  
8. **Always include physician disclaimer**  
   * Reinforce client responsibility for medical oversight.

---

## **STANDARD SUPPLEMENT FLOW (ALWAYS FOLLOW)**

1. Supplements to Pause or Adjust  
2. Active Supplements (with purpose \+ duration)  
3. Titration Schedule (if applicable)  
4. Re-evaluation Criteria

## **SUPPLEMENT → PATTERN → ACTION MAPPING RULES**

### **1️⃣ BLOOD SUGAR / PCOS SUPPORT**

**Trigger Signals**

* PCOS pattern  
* Elevated androgens  
* Blood sugar dysregulation  
* Cravings or energy crashes

**Primary Supplement Category**

* Inositol-based support

**Standard Mapping Language**

* “Supports insulin sensitivity and ovarian signaling.”  
* “May help reduce androgen activity when paired with nutrition.”

**Common Options**

* Inositol (myo \+ d-chiro blend)  
* Omega-3s (as metabolic support)

**Rules**

* Do not combine multiple insulin-modulating supplements unnecessarily  
* Always pair with nutrition changes

---

### **2️⃣ ESTROGEN METABOLISM / CLEARANCE SUPPORT**

**Trigger Signals**

* Slow Phase 2 detox markers  
* Estrogen recirculation symptoms  
* Bloating, cramping, heavy periods

**Primary Supplement Category**

* Phase 2 detox support

**Standard Mapping Language**

* “Supports packaging and elimination of estrogen.”  
* “Used to support detox pathways, not lower estrogen aggressively.”

**Rules**

* Avoid estrogen-lowering agents when estrogen is already low  
* Fiber and food-first emphasis must be present

---

### **3️⃣ PROGESTERONE / OVULATION SUPPORT**

**Trigger Signals**

* Low progesterone  
* Anovulatory cycles  
* Sleep disruption  
* High stress sensitivity

**Primary Supplement Category**

* Cycle-supportive nutrients

**Standard Mapping Language**

* “Supports progesterone pathways and cycle regulation.”  
* “Used alongside stress and blood sugar support.”

**Rules**

* Ovulation support \> direct hormone suppression  
* Avoid stacking conflicting hormone-active supplements

---

### **4️⃣ ANDROGEN MODULATION**

**Trigger Signals**

* Acne (jawline, chin)  
* Hair shedding  
* Elevated androgen labs  
* PCOS pattern

**Primary Supplement Category**

* Androgen-modulating botanicals or nutrients

**Standard Mapping Language**

* “May help reduce androgen activity.”  
* “Supportive, not suppressive.”

**Rules**

* Avoid aggressive suppression  
* Pair with blood sugar and stress strategies

---

### **5️⃣ CORTISOL / STRESS METABOLISM**

**Trigger Signals**

* Poor stress tolerance  
* Sleep disruption  
* Low cortisol clearance markers

**Primary Supplement Category**

* Adaptogenic or metabolic cortisol support

**Standard Mapping Language**

* “Supports cortisol metabolism and stress resilience.”  
* “Not a stimulant or sedative.”

**Rules**

* Lifestyle interventions must be listed alongside supplements  
* Avoid stimulating compounds when sleep is disrupted

---

### **6️⃣ GUT & DIGESTIVE SUPPORT**

**Trigger Signals**

* Bloating  
* Gas  
* GI discomfort  
* Elimination diet in place

**Primary Supplement Category**

* Digestive support and microbiome balance

**Standard Mapping Language**

* “Supports gut barrier and microbiome balance.”  
* “Used temporarily while digestion improves.”

**Rules**

* Time-bound usage preferred  
* Re-evaluate after 8–12 weeks

---

### **7️⃣ DETOX / ANTIOXIDANT SUPPORT**

**Trigger Signals**

* Low glutathione markers  
* Oxidative stress patterns  
* Inflammation

**Primary Supplement Category**

* Antioxidant precursors

**Standard Mapping Language**

* “Supports antioxidant capacity and detoxification.”  
* “Used gently to avoid overload.”

**Rules**

* Pair with hydration and fiber  
* Avoid aggressive detox language

## **DURATION & RE-EVALUATION RULES**

* **Ongoing:** foundational support (e.g., omega-3s)  
* **Time-bound:** 6–12 weeks for targeted protocols  
* **Re-evaluate:** symptoms, tolerance, and labs before continuing

AI must never imply indefinite use without reassessment.

---

## **CLIENT LANGUAGE RULES**

AI must:

* Use neutral, supportive language  
* Normalize that supplements may not be permanent  
* Reinforce that reactions or intolerance \= data, not failure

---

## **REQUIRED DISCLAIMER (STANDARD)**

All supplement recommendations must include:

“All supplement suggestions are for educational purposes only and are not intended to replace medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult your healthcare provider before starting, stopping, or changing any supplement or medication.”
